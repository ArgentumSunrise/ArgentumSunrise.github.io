About Us

Rising Sun is one of America's oldest and most storied airlines. We have more history on board our flights and in our terminals than any other airline, and that history lends us an air of sophistication and industry that really shows in how we operate. Rising Sun Airlines began in 1929, when our founder, Jared Carr, strapped a pair of cardboard wings to his arms and jumped off the roof of his house. Inspired by the experience (as well as the severe brain damage), Jared went to work on his own plane as soon as he left the hospital. His first invention was the four-wing biplane, which is still Rising Sun's standard model today. Over the years, as our company and casualty list blossomed, we built up an international identity, with fleets of airplanes and one of the most 'trusted' brands in the world, with such notable passengers as John F. Kennedy, the Dalai Lama, Elvis Presley, and Michael Jordan. Flying Rising Sun is like standing on the shoulders of giants. And then falling off. Into the ocean.
